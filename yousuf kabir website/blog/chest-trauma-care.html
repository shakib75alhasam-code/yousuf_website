What is Chest Trauma?

Chest trauma occurs when the chest region, including ribs, lungs, heart, or major blood vessels, is injured due to accidents, falls, or blunt/penetrating injuries. Immediate care by a thoracic surgeon in Dhaka is critical.

Causes of Chest Trauma

  • Road Accidents: High-speed collisions are common causes.
  • Falls: From height causing rib fractures or lung injuries.
  • Sports Injuries: Contact sports or accidents.
  • Assaults: Blunt or sharp trauma.
  • Workplace Accidents: Industrial or construction injuries.

Symptoms to Watch For

  • Severe chest pain
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Visible bruising or deformity
  • Coughing up blood
  • Rapid heart rate or shock in severe cases

Treatment Options

  • Emergency Care: Stabilization of breathing and circulation.
  • Medication: Pain control, antibiotics, anti-inflammatory drugs.
  • Surgery: Rib fixation, lung repair, or internal bleeding management.
  • Minimally Invasive: VATS (video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery).
  • Rehabilitation: Respiratory physiotherapy and monitoring.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do immediately after chest trauma?
Seek emergency medical care immediately. Stabilize breathing and avoid movement of injured area until professional care arrives.
Can minor chest injuries become serious?
Yes, internal bleeding or lung collapse can occur even with minor external injuries. Monitoring is essential.
Is surgery always required?
No, minor injuries may only need observation and medication. Surgery is for severe cases like rib fractures or internal organ damage.
