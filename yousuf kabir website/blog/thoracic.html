Lung Cancer: Causes, Prevention & Treatment

Published: Aug 31, 2025

What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer is one of the most common and serious types of cancer worldwide. It occurs when abnormal cells in the lungs grow uncontrollably, forming tumors. Early detection is critical for effective treatment and better survival rates.

Causes of Lung Cancer

  • Smoking: The leading cause of lung cancer. Cigarette smoke contains harmful chemicals that damage lung cells.
  • Secondhand Smoke: Exposure to smoke from others can also increase the risk.
  • Environmental Factors: Long-term exposure to air pollution, asbestos, radon, and harmful chemicals.
  • Genetic Factors: Family history of lung cancer may increase susceptibility.
  • Chronic Lung Diseases: Conditions like COPD and pulmonary fibrosis may increase risk.

Prevention Tips

Preventing lung cancer involves lifestyle choices and early awareness:

  • Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke.
  • Maintain a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables.
  • Exercise regularly to strengthen lung health.
  • Minimize exposure to environmental pollutants and harmful chemicals.
  • Regular medical checkups, especially if you have risk factors.

Early Detection & Symptoms

Early signs of lung cancer may include persistent cough, shortness of breath, chest pain, unexplained weight loss, and coughing up blood. Screening with low-dose CT scans is recommended for high-risk individuals.
